## Project Overview
In the highly competitive Food & Beverage (FnB) industry, bakery stores need to go beyond product quality and start understanding customer purchasing behavior to maximize sales opportunities. This project focuses on analyzing transactional data from a bakery store to uncover hidden product relationships and customer buying patterns using **Market Basket Analysis**.

As a **Data Analyst**, the goal is to identify product combinations, purchasing trends, and behavioral insights that can support better marketing strategies, bundling, and store optimization.

## Problem Statement
Customers often purchase multiple items in a single transaction, but these natural buying patterns are not fully leveraged by the business. Without proper analysis, opportunities for cross-selling, bundling, and revenue optimization may be missed.

A data-driven approach is required to identify:
- Which products are frequently purchased together
- How customer behavior changes by time and day
- How product relationships can be used for business strategy

## Objectives
### 1. Understand Customer Behavior
Identify customer purchasing patterns and most frequently bought products to understand consumption behavior and trends.
### 2. Discover Product Relationships
Use Market Basket Analysis to identify product associations and combinations that frequently occur in the same transaction.
### 3. Generate Business Insights
Translate findings into actionable insights to support marketing, bundling strategies, and store operations.

## Key Insights
### Product Performance
- Top-selling products: **Coffee, Bread, Tea**
- These items act as core demand drivers and appear consistently across different time periods.

### Time-Based Behavior
- Peak transactions occur on **Saturdays**
- Highest activity is during **12–2 PM (lunch peak hours)**
- Midweek (especially Wednesday) shows lower transaction volume
- Evening and night periods have lower but niche-driven purchases

### Customer Purchasing Patterns
- Coffee is the **anchor product**, frequently driving multi-item purchases
- Strong associations found between:
  - Coffee ↔ Bread / Pastry / Toast
  - Tea ↔ Cake / Sandwich
  - Hot Chocolate ↔ Cake
- Customers tend to follow predictable pairing behavior based on time of day

### Market Basket Insights
- Tea, Coffee, and Hot Chocolate are often combined with Cake
- Coffee is commonly paired with light meals such as Bread and Pastry
- Sandwiches are frequently purchased with Tea
- Sweet and warm combinations dominate afternoon consumption behavior

## Business Recommendations
### 1. Product Bundling Strategy
- **Morning Trio:** Coffee + Bread + Pastry  
- **Afternoon Bundle:** Tea + Coffee + Cake  
- **Sweet Combo:** Hot Chocolate + Cake  
- **Tea Time Duo:** Tea + Cake  
- **Breakfast Bundle:** Coffee + Toast  

### 2. Cross-Selling Strategy
- Recommend Coffee with Pastry, Toast, Medialuna, and Spanish Brunch
- Suggest Tea or Coffee when customers buy Sandwich or Toast
- Promote Cake as an add-on for Coffee and Tea purchases

### 3. Store Layout Optimization
- Place **Coffee as the central anchor product**
- Position Cake near Tea and Coffee to encourage add-ons
- Group Toast, Sandwich, and Pastry near beverage sections
- Separate niche items (seasonal or specialty products) in dedicated areas

### 4. Operational Strategy
- Ensure high stock availability for Coffee, Bread, Tea, and Cake
- Increase staffing during peak hours (12–1 PM and weekends)
- Launch weekday upselling campaigns (Thursday–Friday)
- Optimize evening menu for niche and low-volume products

## Output
- Market Basket Analysis model
- Customer purchasing behavior insights
